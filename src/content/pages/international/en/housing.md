Housing stability directly impacts doctoral execution. The objective is to secure reliable accommodation and maintain clean documentation for downstream administrative steps.

## First 7 days checklist

- Confirm temporary housing and keep written proof.
- Validate campus commute feasibility.
- Estimate full monthly housing cost.
- Prepare commonly requested rental documents.
- Record residence rules and emergency contacts.
- Align declared address with ongoing admin files.
- Digitize all relevant contracts and receipts.

## First 30 days checklist

- Secure stable housing or a documented transition plan.
- Configure related contracts where required.
- Keep address evidence consistent across admin processes.
- Archive lease, receipts and attestations.
- Build a 3-month housing budget.
- Integrate housing cost into overall doctoral planning.
- Review official aid information if eligible.

## Vocabulary glossary (FR term -> EN explanation)

- **Bail**: lease agreement.
- **Quittance**: rent receipt used as address proof.
- **Caution / garant**: guarantor mechanism.
- **Assurance habitation**: home insurance.
- **État des lieux**: move-in condition report.
- **Justificatif de domicile**: proof of address.

## Practical example

If permanent housing is delayed, secure a documented temporary solution for 4 to 6 weeks to avoid blocking enrollment and other processes.

For legal specifics and up-to-date requirements, rely on official local sources.
