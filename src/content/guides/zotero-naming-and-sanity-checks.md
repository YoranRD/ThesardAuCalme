A bibliography manager only saves time when naming and quality controls are explicit. This guide proposes a compact governance model for Zotero in doctoral work.

## Naming baseline

- collection prefixes by chapter domain,
- status tags kept to four values,
- attachment naming with `AuthorYear_Keyword`.

## Weekly checks

- duplicate review,
- missing metadata scan,
- status tag consistency,
- one export smoke test.

## Monthly checks

- random audit of twenty entries,
- verification of PDF attachment presence,
- citation style consistency in a draft section.

## Why this works

Small recurring controls prevent bibliography debt from accumulating silently.

## FAQ

### How many tags are reasonable?

Use as few as possible. A narrow tag vocabulary improves retrieval and consistency.

### Should teams share one library?

Shared libraries can work well if one quality owner maintains standards and review cadence.

### What is the critical failure mode?

Unverified imports. Metadata errors propagate quickly into chapter drafts.
