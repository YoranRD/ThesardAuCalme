Starting a PhD in France as an international candidate usually means managing multiple systems at once: doctoral school requirements, lab integration, enrollment, residence process, health coverage, housing and banking. The challenge is not a lack of information. The challenge is sequencing.

This guide offers a practical order of operations for the first 30 days. It is general guidance only. Local institutional and official government instructions always take priority.

## Core terminology

- **Doctoral school**: structure supervising doctoral requirements and milestones.
- **Laboratory**: research unit where day-to-day scientific work happens.
- **Administrative enrollment**: formal registration with the institution.
- **Doctoral contract**: employment contract for funded PhD positions when applicable.
- **Residence permit process**: legal stay framework and renewals.
- **Health coverage**: entry into the French healthcare system and complementary insurance options.

## Days 1-7: secure essentials

1. Build a document folder (digital + paper): passport, visa/status papers, admission letter, contract/funding proof, temporary housing proof.
2. Contact the doctoral school administrative office and verify required enrollment documents.
3. Activate institutional access (email, campus platform, library tools).

Expected output by day 7: all identity/status documents consolidated and a clear enrollment checklist.

## Days 8-15: enrollment and banking

1. Complete administrative enrollment and keep receipts.
2. Open a bank account if required for salary, stipend or reimbursements.
3. Stabilize housing documents (lease, temporary certificate, utility proof where relevant).

Expected output by day 15: enrollment status known, financial operations possible, address documentation organized.

## Days 16-23: health coverage

1. Start or verify health coverage procedure according to status.
2. Review complementary insurance options if needed.
3. Identify practical local health points (doctor, pharmacy, emergency numbers).

Expected output by day 23: health onboarding in progress with traceable documentation.

## Days 24-30: academic stabilization

1. Hold a scientific framing meeting with supervision.
2. Define first 90-day deliverables (reading map, first section draft, methodology checkpoint).
3. Set up minimal workflow tools: writing space, bibliography manager, backup routine.
4. Create one deadline register for admin + scientific milestones.

Expected output by day 30: administrative continuity + scientific trajectory.

## Suggested first-month rhythm

- Two short administrative sessions per week.
- Three focused scientific sessions per week.
- One weekly review of pending deadlines.

This balance prevents admin overload while protecting research momentum.

## FAQ

### Does this checklist replace official legal guidance?

No. This checklist structures priorities. Official institutional and government guidance remains the reference.

### Should housing be completed before everything else?

Some procedures depend on proof of address, but exact order varies by local context.

### What is the minimum viable academic setup in month one?

A stable writing environment, one bibliography system, one backup strategy and one agreed communication rhythm with supervision.

## Final quick checklist

- Enrollment status clarified.
- Residence process timeline documented.
- Banking setup active.
- Health coverage procedure initiated.
- 90-day academic plan approved.
- Weekly routine running.

A structured first month reduces risk and creates early stability for the full doctorate period.
